First Call Checklist

Everything to capture on the first client conversation. Cover all five sections before you end the call — this becomes the foundation of your engagement proposal.

~30 min call 4 team members IAM · PAM · DPDP
1 — Client Context
Understand the org before you pitch anything
6 items
Company name, industry, and headquarters location Critical
Confirms DPDP applicability and which sector playbook to use
Number of employees and approximate user count
Sizing: under 500 = SMB scope, 500–2000 = mid-market, above = escalate
Who is on the call — title and decision-making authority Critical
CISO or CTO = decision maker. IT Manager = influencer. Know the chain.
What triggered this conversation? (audit, incident, compliance deadline, board push)
The trigger determines urgency and budget availability
Current IT/security team size
Determines how much hand-holding the engagement needs
Do they have an existing IAM tool or vendor? Critical
Greenfield vs migration changes the entire engagement scope and pricing
2 — Current State Assessment
Find the actual pain, not just the stated problem
7 items
How are user accounts provisioned and deprovisioned today? Critical
Manual = immediate win for IdentArk. Semi-automated = mid-complexity. Fully automated = probe for gaps.
Are there orphaned or dormant accounts they know about?
Most say yes. This is your opening for access certification pitch.
How is privileged access (admin accounts) managed? Critical
Shared admin passwords = PAM opportunity. No MFA on privileged accounts = critical gap.
What applications are in scope? (SaaS, on-prem, cloud)
Workday, SAP, Salesforce = known connectors. Custom apps = scoping needed.
Do they have SSO implemented? Which IdP?
Entra ID / Okta already in place reduces implementation effort significantly
Have they had an access-related security incident in the last 12 months?
If yes — this becomes the anchor story for the proposal
Are access reviews done? How often and by whom? DPDP
Under DPDP, this is a formal requirement. No process here = easy compliance hook.
3 — DPDP & Compliance Readiness
The ₹250 crore question
5 items
Are they aware of the DPDP Act and their obligations? DPDP Critical
If not, this becomes an education moment — and your differentiator
Do they process personal data of Indian citizens? DPDP
Confirms DPDP applicability. Most B2C companies and fintech/health = yes.
Have they appointed a Data Protection Officer (DPO)?
No DPO = early-stage compliance maturity. Expect more hand-holding.
Are there other compliance frameworks in play? (ISO 27001, SOC 2, RBI, SEBI) DPDP
Overlapping frameworks = larger scope, higher budget ceiling
Is there a compliance deadline driving this engagement? Critical
Deadline = urgency = faster close. No deadline = longer sales cycle.
4 — Budget & Decision Process
Qualify before you scope
5 items
Is budget allocated for this project? Rough range? Critical
No budget = education/nurture play, not a live deal. Don't spend days on a proposal.
Who signs off on the purchase decision?
If not on this call, ask for an intro or a second call with that person
Have they evaluated or spoken to other vendors?
If yes — ask which ones. Knowing competitors helps you position IdentArk correctly.
What does their procurement/vendor onboarding process look like?
Some orgs have 60-day vendor approval processes. Know this upfront.
What is their ideal timeline to start? Critical
"ASAP" = hot lead. "Next quarter" = nurture. "No idea" = they're still exploring.
5 — Close the Call
Never leave without a committed next action
4 items
Summarise the 2–3 key pain points heard on the call Critical
Say it back to them. Confirms alignment and shows you listened.
Agree on a specific next step with a date (proposal, discovery workshop, demo)
Vague next steps = deals that go cold. Lock in a date before you hang up.
Send follow-up email within 2 hours with notes + next step confirmed Critical
Speed signals professionalism. Most consultants send it next day — you send it same hour.
Ask for referrals or introductions if engagement fit is low Optional
A bad-fit lead can still become your best referral source
