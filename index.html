DPDP Act 2023

India's Data Privacy Law
Starts with Identity

The Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 imposes strict obligations on how organisations collect, process, and store personal data. Non-compliance carries penalties up to ₹250 crore — and IAM is the enforcement layer that makes compliance operationally real.

IdentArk maps your identity architecture to DPDP requirements: purpose limitation, data principal rights, consent management, and breach notification — all tied back to who has access to what data and when.

Data Minimisation

Access scoped to minimum necessary data. Role-based controls enforced at the identity layer.

Consent Linkage

Identity tokens carry consent context, ensuring processing only happens when consent is valid.

Right to Erasure

Automated deprovisioning workflows triggered by consent withdrawal or data deletion requests.

Breach Notification

Identity audit trails provide the access forensics needed for 72-hour breach reporting.
